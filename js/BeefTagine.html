Classic Lamb Tagine

A slow-cooked Moroccan stew balancing savory meats with warm spices and sweet fruit.

0 of 14 ingredients prepped

Ingredients Breakdown

1. The Base

  • 2 lbs lamb shoulder, cubed
  • 1 large yellow onion, grated
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cups beef or vegetable broth

2. The Spice Profile

  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp paprika
  • Pinch of saffron threads

3. Texture & Garnish

  • 1/2 cup dried apricots
  • 1/2 cup pitted prunes
  • 1/4 cup toasted almonds
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Cooking Instructions

  1. Sear the Base: Heat olive oil in a heavy-bottomed pot or traditional tagine. Sear the lamb cubes on all sides until browned. Remove and set aside.
  2. Build the Flavor: In the same pot, add the grated onion and garlic. Sauté until translucent. Stir in the ginger, cumin, cinnamon, paprika, and saffron. Cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Simmer: Return the lamb to the pot. Pour in the broth and bring to a gentle boil. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 1.5 hours.
  4. Add Sweetness: Stir in the apricots and prunes. Cover and simmer for another 30 minutes, or until the lamb is tender and the sauce has thickened.
  5. Garnish and Serve: Top with toasted almonds and fresh cilantro. Serve hot alongside traditional steamed couscous.
