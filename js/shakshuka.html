Sizzling Shakshuka

A vibrant, one-pan dish of eggs gently poached in a robust, spiced tomato and bell pepper sauce.

0 of 13 ingredients prepped

Ingredients Breakdown

1. The Sauce Base

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes

2. The Spice Profile

  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp sweet paprika
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

3. The Finish

  • 4 to 6 large eggs
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese (optional)
  • Crusty bread or pita for serving

Cooking Instructions

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: Heat the olive oil in a large, deep skillet (cast iron is perfect) over medium heat. Add the diced onion and bell pepper. Cook for about 5-7 minutes until the onions become translucent and soft.
  2. Bloom the Spices: Add the minced garlic, cumin, paprika, and cayenne pepper. Stir continuously for 1 minute until highly fragrant. Do not let the garlic burn.
  3. Simmer the Sauce: Pour in the crushed tomatoes and season with salt and pepper. Bring the sauce to a gentle simmer. Reduce the heat to low and let it cook for 10-15 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken slightly and the flavors to meld.
  4. Poach the Eggs: Use the back of a spoon to make small wells in the sauce. Carefully crack an egg into each well. Cover the skillet with a lid and cook for 5-8 minutes, or until the egg whites are set but the yolks are still slightly runny.
  5. Garnish and Serve: Remove from heat. Sprinkle the top with fresh cilantro or parsley and crumbled feta cheese. Serve immediately, straight from the skillet, with plenty of crusty bread for dipping.
